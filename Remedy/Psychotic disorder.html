Children and Adolescents (Under 18):

Early intervention with therapy and medication is crucial. Encourage a supportive environment with clear routines and structure. Involve family members in treatment planning and support. Provide education and resources to parents and caregivers.

For Young Adults (18-30):

Encourage adherence to prescribed medications and therapy sessions. Foster independence while providing a supportive network. Promote healthy lifestyle habits, including regular exercise and nutrition. Offer vocational and educational support to facilitate integration into society.

Adults (31-65):

Ensure consistent follow-up with mental health professionals. Provide support in managing daily stressors and responsibilities. Encourage participation in support groups or peer networks. Address any co-occurring substance abuse issues through integrated treatment approaches.

Seniors (65+):

Monitor medication adherence and potential side effects closely. Create a safe and supportive living environment. Offer opportunities for social engagement and meaningful activities. Coordinate care with healthcare providers to address age-related concerns and comorbidities.
