Children and Adolescents (Under 18):

Encourage open communication and validate their feelings.Teach relaxation techniques like deep breathing or mindfulness.Establish a consistent routine to provide stability and predictability.Consider therapy options like cognitive-behavioral therapy tailored for their age.

For Young Adults (18-30):

Practice self-care activities such as exercise, adequate sleep, and healthy eating habits.Engage in stress-relief hobbies like art, music, or journaling.Seek support from friends, family, or support groups.Explore therapy options like counseling or online therapy platforms.

Adults (31-65):

Prioritize stress management through regular exercise and meditation. Set realistic goals and break tasks into smaller, manageable steps. Maintain a healthy work-life balance to prevent burnout. Consider therapy or medication under the guidance of a mental health professional.

Seniors (65+):

Stay socially active by participating in community events or clubs. Practice relaxation techniques like tai chi or gentle yoga. Foster connections with loved ones and share experiences. Seek professional help from a therapist specializing in geriatric mental health.
